introduction of mikado pheasants


Cultural Significance

The Mikado Pheasant is a cultural icon and the unofficial bird.

The image of the Mikado Pheasant can be found on the NT$1000.

Appearance

Male: Males are bigger in size, and have vibrant blue-colored plumage and long tail.

Female: Females are smaller and lighter in appearance.They have brown plumage, which serves as great camouflage to blend.

Distribution and Habitat

Distribution: Their distribution is in the mountain ranges of central and southern Taiwan.

Habitat: They live in steep slopes or foggy dense forests in mountain regions at middle to high altitudes.

Behavior

They are most active at dawn and dusk especially after it rains or when it drizzles gliding down the mountainside.

They can fly fast but only for short distances, they prefer to run or glide.Most of the time, they don't fly.But they like to perch in the tree at night to avoid predators.

Breeding

They build their nest with dead branches, fallen leaves, dry grass and feathers in the tree holes under rocks.

Females are solely responsible for breeding up the young.

Feeding

The pheasants have a feeding habit that is similar to chickens.They walk while foraging for food.

Their diet includes: fruit, leaves, vegetation, seed, and insects.
