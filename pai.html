introduction of paiwan slate houses


General description and Slate house

Two tribes, Paiwan, Rukai live mainly in Pingtung, southern Taiwan and share similar culture.

They live by rivers.There are a lot of stones there, so they make good use of natural resources to build their houses.

House of a Paiwan chief

It's very big and there are many beautiful ornaments and carves.In the yard, there is a stone tablet.It's a symbol of social status.

Social Structure & Residential Situation

The Paiwan tribe has a strict social hierarchy--leaders, nobles, warriors and civilians.Tribal members inherit their social status at birth.

Social hierarchy is reflected in various features of the house.

Front yard of the slate house

A platform in the front yard to issue public orders and host harvest festivals.

A stone tablet standing there, with hundred pacer snakes carved on it.
Banyan tree-symbol of the chief's honor and prosperity.

Low entrance door

The small door only allows one person to enter at one time.That is for enemy prevention.

Besides, the guests show respect by bowing their heads as they enter the house through the door.

Pottery vessels

Pottery vessels represent the place where ancestors once lived and it's the symbol of social status;they are owned by chiefs and nobles.

There are male and female pottery vessels, the female one has bumps on it.Pottery vessel also serves as ritual objects.
