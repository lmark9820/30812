introduction of Tao people


The Tao people

The Tao are the indigenous people who inhabit Orchid Island.

The Tao people are also a tribe that is genuinely in great awe of the ocean.

Flying fish season

Every year from March to June, the waters around Orchid Island are frequented by schools of flying fish brought by the Kuroshio Current.

This period of time, also known as the flying fish season, has become the principal fishing season for the Tao.

Set sail to fish

Before the fishing groups set sail to fish, the members utilize their exceptional wood-carving skills to build plank boats(Tatala.)

Boat launching ceremony- With young men wearing loincloths and encircling the designated boat, performing rituals to guard against evil spirits and throwing the boat into the air several times.

Tao cultural

The Tao people take flying fish as a gift from their god, so the occasion of catching flying has been highly valued

Traditionally, during the flying season, tribesmen from different families have to form fishing groups and live together, following numerous rules about diet and living.

Fishing boat (Tatala)

In order to ward off evil spirits and pray for blessedness, they paint certain totems on their boats, including shapes of human, waves, and concentric circles.

Each boat uses 21 or 27 planks, and the colors on the boats are all from natural resources.

Three kinds of fish

Best fish : Best fish is milder and it's for women and children to eat.
Second best fish : Second best fish is fatty and it's for men to eat.
Bad fish : Bad fish is for the elderly, because they had eaten good fish when they were young.
